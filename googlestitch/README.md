# ⛳ Putt-Putt Score Tracker

## 📌 Overview

Recently, I went and played putt-putt with my cousin, and something kind of funny (but also frustrating) happened…

We were still using a paper scorecard.

By the end of the game, the biggest question was:
**“Who actually won?”**

The problem?  
No one could read the scorekeeper’s handwriting well enough to accurately tally the scores 😅

On top of that, another question kept coming up almost every hole:
**“Wait… who goes next?”**

As beginner players who weren’t super familiar with the rules, we kept second-guessing the order of play.

That experience led to this project.

---

## 🎯 The Problem

- Paper scorecards are easy to lose or misread  
- Handwriting can make final scores unclear  
- No automatic total calculation  
- Players don’t always know the correct turn order  
- Courses vary, but there’s no easy way to reuse course info  

---

## 💡 The Solution

The **Putt-Putt Score Tracker** is a simple app designed to replace the traditional paper tally system and make the game smoother, especially for casual and beginner players.

---

## 🚀 Features (Based on User Stories)

### 🧍 Player Setup
- Add up to 6 players
- Store and display player names before the game starts

---

### ⛳ Score Tracking
- Display par for each hole
- Enter scores as you play
- Automatically keep a running total for each player

---

### 🔄 Turn Order Guidance
- Automatically determine who goes next after each hole
- Clearly display the next player
- Helps beginners follow the correct rules without confusion

---

### 🗺️ Course Setup & Save
- Enter number of holes for a course
- Set par for each hole
- Save courses for future use
- Select previously saved courses without re-entering data

---

### 🏆 Final Score Ranking
- Display all player scores at the end of the game
- Sort players from lowest (best) score to highest
- Clearly show the winner
- Handle ties correctly

---

## 🎮 Goal

Make putt-putt:
- Easier to track  
- More beginner-friendly  
- Less confusing  
- And way more fun  

No more guessing who won.  
No more squinting at messy handwriting.  
No more arguing about who goes next.
